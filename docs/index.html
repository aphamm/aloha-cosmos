Abstract

We demonstrate the effectiveness of Low-Rank Adaptation (LoRA) fine-tuning on NVIDIA's Cosmos Predict2 Video2World model for robot manipulation tasks. By training on domain-specific robotics data, the LoRA-adapted model generates more realistic and task-coherent video predictions compared to the base model. The fine-tuned model shows improved understanding of robotic actions, object interactions, and physical plausibility in manipulation scenarios.
